Tokyo

Tokyo is a city of vibrant contrasts—where tradition meets cutting-edge innovation. Its design scene is rich with inspiration, from minimalist architecture and fashion subcultures to futuristic tech and bold visual storytelling. The city moves with precision and purpose, pushing creatives to think differently and refine their craft. I’d love to experience Tokyo as a designer, but only when I feel ready to meet its high standards.


Sidney

Sydney pulses with sun, art, and modern design. Its vibrant creative scene—spanning architecture, fashion, and digital media—is as lively as its beaches and city life. The mix of global influence and local identity makes it a unique place for designers to explore bold ideas. I’d love to design in Sydney someday, once I feel grounded in my skills and ready for new challenges.


Seattle

Seattle blends natural beauty with innovation. Known for its tech scene and strong design culture, the city fosters creativity in everything from UX/UI to sustainable design. There's a calmness beneath the surface hustle, and it’s the kind of place where thoughtful, impactful work can thrive. I’d love to grow as a designer there, especially as I develop more confidence in my creative voice.

New York

New York feels like the heart of creativity and chaos. "Sex and the City" captures its fast pace, and the city's artistic energy is everywhere—from fashion shows to gallery openings. It’s a place that challenges and inspires designers across fields like advertising, digital media, and animation. I’d love to experience it as a designer—once I’m truly confident in my skills.

Singapore

Singapore is one of Southeast Asia's leading tech and design capitals. It blends precision, innovation, and cultural diversity, making it a fascinating place to work in UX/UI, branding, or motion design. It's also a visually stunning city - and to be honest, I can't help but think of "Crazy Rich Asians" every time it comes to mind.
